Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani

Music Director: Pritam
Vocals: Benny Dayal, Shefali Alvares, Vishal Dadlani, Shalmali Kholgade, Ariit Singh, Tichi Raina, Rekha Bhardwaj, Sunidhi Chauhan, Sreeram;
Lyrics: Kumaar, Amitabh Bhattacharya.

Badtameez Dil...

Sung by Benny Dayal and Shefali Alvaris. Get ready to groove to the number as its fast beats will definitely pull you to the dance floor! The young, peppy and crazy tune justifies the title of the movie. The peppy lyrics are in sync with the mood. Thoroughly enjyable.

Balam Pichkari...

Nowadays Holi songs are a rarity and "Balam Pichkari", sung by Vishal Dadlani and Shalmali Kholgade, is one of those rare songs. The naughty number would make you dance. The lyrics are witty. It starts on a high note and ends on a high note. A must hear!

Dilliwaali Girlfriend...

Sung by Arijit Singh and Sunidhi Chauhan. It may not be as perfect as the first two numbers, but the element of fun and naughitiness is intact in this one too! If the title has the word Dilli in it, then Punjabi element has to be there. The rap, in the beginning of the song, is impressive and lyrics are fun. However after a point it looses its grip.

Gargra...

An interesting duet by Rekha Bhardwaj and Vishal Dadlani. It has a touch of local 'tamasha' and Rekha's voice sings the song in her typical way. With whistles and ghungroo sounds, the singers make it a fun number. The lyrics are worth paying attention to.

Ilahi...

After all these dance tracks, the album takes a regional turn with "Ilahi". A simple song, sung by Arijit. It has a chorus by children singing in background brings freshness to it. There is another version version of the song called "Ilahi reprised". It's faster than the original, otherwise the rest of the elements are similar to the original.

Kabira...

Rekha Bharadwaj nad Tochi Raina come together for "Kabira". The music is liberating Tochi sings beautifully. It's a kind of song that grows on you. Not a very long number , it keeps the essenceof the song intact. Another version of the song is "Kabira Encore" . Sung by Harshdeep Kaur and Arijit, it is way far better than the previous one. Set in the backdrop of a wedding, it is blissfully rhythemic and beautiful. You would want to hear the song again and again for the way Arijit has sung it.

Subhanallah...

A usual romantic number, talking about the beauty of a woman. It will woo diehard romantics. Sreeram takes the mike for the song, which may not appeal so much to others.
